Travel Tips



Don't be afraid to use a map

Looking like a tourist isn’t as bad as getting really lost and ending up in the wrong neighborhood. Don’t be afraid to ask for directions or use a map and look like a fabulous tourist! I always use a map when I travel, its helps you get where you need to go!



Don't fly direct

When you book flights, usually it is cheaper to fly to airports close to your final destination. Then travel on a train or bus to get to where you need to go. It also provides a rest for those struggling with travel sickness on planes. So remember to shop around for your flights to ensure you get the cheapest and most affordable option, flying direct isn’t always the cheapest.



When getting into a hotel don’t be afraid to ask for an upgrade

Hotel’s tend to have a lot of flexibility when it comes to assigning upgrades at check-in. It never hurts to ask. Just be super nice and you never know how lucky you can get! This is also the same for flights, don't be scared to asked the person at the check in desk for an upgrade as most flights have spare seats in higher classes.



Buy a small suitcase

Buying a small backpack forces you to pack light and avoid carrying too much stuff! We all have a natural tendency to want to fill space. Then you won’t end up having to carry a heavy suitcase and regret it. This is also SUPER nice if you are moving around quite frequently as most small cases can be carried by hand and won't be annoying on trains or buses.



Libraries, restaurants and most cafes have free WI-FI

If you are staying at a place which charges you to connect to the internet or if you are out and about and don’t have 3G, most cafes, restaurants and libraries have wifi. Starbucks is a personal favourite, their wifi is usually free with no extra charge (not forcing you to buy some of their goods).
