New York


Welcome to the bright lights of the Big Apple!


New York is brimming with a diverse and beautiful nature, from the talented streets of Broadway to the extensive range of shopping. There is SO much you can do in New York. Take the whirling elevator to the heights of the top of the Empire State building, visit the memorial site of 9/11, or take a boat out to get the best view of the Statue of Liberty.

Know before you go:

I went at Christmas so found out that there are several annual events, that trust me, you do not want to miss or forget about!

  • Central Park hosts an annual ice rink
  • Bring your warmest mittens and scarves
  • Nothing beats a dollar slice of pizza
  • Check out the beautiful Rockefeller Christmas tree
  • Saks on Fifth Avenue lights up at night and does an insane light display

Best time to visit:

I found after visiting New York twice at different times of year and through countless amounts or research I found that the best time is from April to June or September to early November. The weather is warm and pleasant however the tourist crowds are not overwhelming. The cheapest time however to visit New York is on weekends from mid-January to the end of February. However it all depends on the kind of person you are, personally experiencing the big apple during the Christmas period was truly magical, but freezing cold!
